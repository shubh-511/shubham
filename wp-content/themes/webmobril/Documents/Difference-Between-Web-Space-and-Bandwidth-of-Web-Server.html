Difference Between Web Space and Bandwidth of Web Server

If you’re looking for a web hosting service plan, you should have at least some knowledge of basics so that you can opt a better choice, which suits your requirements. There are several components your choice should depend upon but the two terms which people, generally from non-technical background, find a little confusing are ‘Storage Space’ and ‘Bandwidth’.

Let’s have a keen look at both terms covering entirely about what they are and why you need them.

Storage Space

To put a website on the internet you need a certain amount of storage, where your site’s files are kept on the server. The server makes it available for visitors through the internet. These website files consist of HTML files, images, and website scripts. Also, it gives you a cover space to back up web page files to keep them safe.

It’s subjected to your web hosting plan how much storage space will get and the storage space is scalable or not. Scalable hosting service are the prominent excellent for growing businesses.

Bandwidth

Bandwidth is the amount of data transfer that happens between the user and the website. The total bandwidth value requirement varies according to the functionality as well as the popularity of the website. To understand why you need it, suppose you open a website, your computer will first download the site files from the server before displaying it. In that process, the size of files downloaded is the amount of bandwidth used. The number of visitors on the website increases the requirement of bandwidth.

Comparing both in accordance with the size, bandwidth should be greater than provided storage space. So, if you have a round estimate of the amount of traffic you’re expecting, it becomes ways easier to decide your web hosting plan, Storage Space and bandwidth.
